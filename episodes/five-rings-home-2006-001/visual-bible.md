# Visual Bible: 响五声就好

## Production Mode

Pure comic Douyin carousel. 11 story panels plus one optional cover.

## Art Direction

A nostalgic 2006 Chinese family memory, told through quiet physical details: the landline receiver, the phone card, coins, vegetables, winter booth glass, and the mother's hand hovering over the phone.

The images should look emotionally cinematic but grounded, like a warm graphic novel rather than a glossy illustration.

## Composition Rules

- Vertical 9:16 for every image.
- Leave clean upper-left or upper-middle space for post-rendered Chinese captions.
- Avoid placing essential story information in the bottom 22% or right 18% of the frame.
- No generated caption text in the image.
- No hard comic grid unless specifically requested; prefer a single full-page scene with memory-like layering.

## Continuity Rules

- The mother should remain recognizable: tied-back black hair, simple sweater/apron, restrained eyes.
- The son should remain recognizable: slim freshman, short black hair, modest clothes, canvas bag or phone card.
- Landline phone should be cream-colored and old-fashioned.
- Era should read as 2006: no smartphones, no QR codes, no modern LED screens, no contemporary branding.

## Cover Direction

Cover title: `响五声就好`

Cover image: warm old home interior, mother standing by the landline, hand suspended above the receiver. A faint visual thread or phone cord can lead toward a cold campus phone booth in the distance or background memory.

## Review Focus

- Does the story feel restrained instead of over-sentimental?
- Are the 2006 objects accurate and visible?
- Can viewers understand the five-ring rule without needing tiny in-image text?
- Is caption space preserved for `douyin-msyh-top` rendering?
